Yuan Longping

The man who saved a billion lives

Here's a timeline of Yuan Longping's life

  • 1930 - Born in Beijing, China
  • 1953 - Graduates from Southwest Agricultural College with a degree in agronomy
  • 1960 - Begins teaching at an agricultural school in Anjiang, Hunan Province
  • 1964 - Discovers a naturally occurring male-sterile rice plant, which becomes the basis for his research in hybrid rice
  • 1973 - Successfully develops the world's first high-yielding hybrid rice strain
  • 1979 - Receives the First-Class National Invention Prize for his breakthrough in hybrid rice
  • 1980 - Appointed Director of the China National Hybrid Rice Research and Development Center
  • 1981 - Elected as a member of the Chinese Academy of Engineering
  • 1995 - Receives the World Food Prize for his contributions to global food security
  • 2001 - Recognized with the Magsaysay Award for his achievements in hybrid rice research
  • 2004 - Elected as a Foreign Associate of the United States National Academy of Sciences
  • 2014 - Celebrates the 40th anniversary of his hybrid rice achievements with a significant increase in global rice production
  • 2021 - Dies at the age of 91
